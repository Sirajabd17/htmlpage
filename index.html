A.P.J Abdul kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile man of India

Dr APJ Abdul Kalam is a household name around the world. He is regarded as one of the top scientists of the twenty-first century. Furthermore, he becomes India’s 11th president and serves his country. He was the most valued person in the country because of his contributions as a scientist and as President. Aside from that, his contribution to the ISRO (Indian Space Research Organization) is outstanding. He oversaw several programmes that benefited society, and he was instrumental in the creation of the Agni and Prithvi missiles. He was dubbed the “Missile Man of India” because of his role in nuclear power in India. During his childhood, his family financial situation was dire at the time, so he began financially supporting his family at a young age. But he never stopped learning. He maintained his studies and graduated while also supporting his family. Above all, he was a participant in the 1998 Pokhran nuclear test

Biographies

  • The complete name of Sir APJ Abdul Kalam is Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
  • He had born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • He was a very hard-working student since his childhood
  • He joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist after graduation.
  • He joined the Indian Space research Organization (ISRO) in 1969.
  • Kalam Sir served as the Project Director of Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle III (PSLV III)./li>
  • His dexterity in space science made him popular as the ‘Missile Man of India’.
  • He was the 11th President of the Republic of India.
  • He took his last breath on 27th July 2015.
